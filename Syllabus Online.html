Course Information

Description

This course is intended to help you gain knowledge and experience in web development by using common development tools. To accomplish this, students will be using Google Chrome and Visual Studio Code (or Adobe Brackets) to learn HTML5, CSS3, and Bootstrap4. Students will also learn many of the ways to enhance website exposure while ensuring accessibility. This course is meant to be fun, informative, as well as challenging.

 

Course Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Create attractive, efficient, and responsive websites.
  • Use HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap effectively.
  • Apply design thinking to guide the development of a website project.
  • Work effectively in a team to plan, develop, and implement web projects.

Course Outcomes

  • Students will develop an experience-based knowledge of their disciplines and demonstrate the ability to apply theories and concepts to practical problems.
  • Students will engage in systematic reflection and demonstrate the ability to critically examine their experiences and to create connections between those experiences and disciplinary knowledge.
  • Students will develop and demonstrate managerial skills including planning, organizing, problem-solving, and communicating.
  • Students make contributions to their communities and learn the value of making these contributions.
  • Students will develop as individuals including understanding the needs of others, learning cultural awareness, and appreciating the differences in others.

Team Project

In this course, you will work as part of a team to develop a professional website. You are being asked to do this as a team to allow you to draw on the experience and expertise of your classmates, making the project more manageable than it would be if you had to do it by yourself. Furthermore, IT professionals work as part of a team. Therefore, the team project will provide you with a realistic experience of working in a team that can help develop your teamwork skills for the future.

To support you in this process, I will ensure that teams are fairly balanced with respect to student background and expertise. I have divided the project into smaller chunks to complete throughout the semester so that you will receive feedback on how you are doing. I will also provide your teams with some time to work during class. You will receive more instructions and guidance after you have been put into your teams. Working independently is not an option.

Academic Integrity/Misconduct

Please review the information on Academic Integrity and Misconduct. Academic integrity is a hallmark of Middle Tennessee State University. We expect students to complete academic exercises, i.e., assignments turned in for credit, that are original and appropriately credit all sources used.

Academic misconduct includes, but is not limited to:

  1. Plagiarism: The adoption or reproduction of ideas, words, statements, images, or works of another person as one’s own without proper attribution. This includes self-plagiarism, which occurs when an author submits material or research from a previous academic exercise to satisfy the requirements of another exercise and uses it without proper citation of its reuse.
  2. Cheating: Using or attempting to use unauthorized materials, information, or study aids in any academic exercise. This includes unapproved collaboration, which occurs when a student works with others on an academic exercise without the express permission of the professor. It also includes purchasing assignments or paying another person to complete a course for you.
  3. Fabrication: Unauthorized falsification or invention of any information or citation in an academic exercise.

Stealing information without proper citation, copying parts of other students’ work, creating information to establish credibility, or using someone else’s thoughts or ideas without appropriate acknowledgment is academic misconduct. If you have a question about an assignment, please ask me to clarify. All cases of academic misconduct will be reported to the Director of Student Academic Ethics and may result in failure on the test/assignment or for the course.

When students participate in behavior that is considered to be a form of academic misconduct, the value of their education and that of their classmates is lessened, and their academic careers are jeopardized.

Students guilty of academic misconduct are immediately responsible to the instructor of the class. In addition to other possible disciplinary sanctions (including expulsion from the university), which may be imposed through the regular institutional procedures as a result of academic misconduct, the instructor has the authority to assign an “F” or zero for an activity or to assign an “F” for the course. Students guilty of plagiarism will be immediately reported to the Director of Student Academic Ethics.

Please note that Turnitin.com may be used to review work for originality. To avoid issues related to this, simply do original work and do not share assignments with anyone.

Never submit someone else's work as your own. Never purchase website content and submit as your own.

Punctuality

You are strongly encouraged to do your work throughout the week. Waiting until even a few hours before the deadline will likely not allow you to complete your work on time. Most modules contain exercises, quizzes, challenges, and assignments. Unless completed throughout the week, students will likely become burned out working through everything in a single session - leading to sloppy work and missed points.

Attendance Reporting
